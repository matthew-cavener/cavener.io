Education

B.Sc. Physics Oklahoma State University 2015

My curiosity led me to pursue a degree in physics, where general problem solving is the key skill.

During my time as an undergrad I carried out novel theoretical and experimental research into Infrared Structural Biology. Structural Biology is a branch of biophysics concerned with understanding how protien function is influenced by its 3D structure, and how molecular interactions affect that structure and function.

My research used Computational Quantum Chemistry to model the FITR spectra of specific amino acid interactions. The calculations were performed on the Oklahoma State University Supercomputer cluster "Cowboy."
